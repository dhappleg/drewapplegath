Google Edge TPU

2019 Google Cloud NEXT - San Francisco, CA

04/09/2019 - 04/11/2019

2019 Google IO - Mountain View, CA

05/03/2019 - 05/10/2019
Interactive Engineer | Onsite Engineer

The goal of this activation was to show off the speed and accuracy of the Edge TPU chip being developed for Google on the Coral Development Board.

My roll in this project was to design and build the mechanical pieces that moved and did the sorting of the pucks. The google TPU would inference and send my program an id for which chute the puck should travel down. From there it was up to the node server running on a custom soldered arduino mega hat to control the flippers. The actual actuation of the large chute was controlled by a custom BLDC motor controlled by a custom calibrated PID and connected to a power supply retrofitted with a power disipation bank of resistors to absorbe back current in the system.

The below video shows the testing of the custom servo motor in the middle of the demonstration. One of the major hurtles of this specific build was that I was unable to find a fast, strong, and cheap enough servo motor to move the almost 4 pount chute.
